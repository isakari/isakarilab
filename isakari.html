専任講師
assistant professor


Hiroshi Isakari was born in Saga, Japan on the 27th September 1984. He received, from Kyoto University, his doctoral degree of engineering for "Periodic FMMs and Calderon's preconditioning in acoustics and elastodynamics" in 2012. After that, he experienced one-month post-doc at graduate school of informatics, Kyoto University, and then got a faculty position at Nagoya University, where he spent 9 years as an assistant professor. He then moved to Keio University. His main research interests lie in computational mechanics. He especially focuses on realising advanced (=fast, reliable, and versatile) numerical methods based on boundary integral equations, and exploring its applications to "optimal" design of novel wave devices such as phononic structures, acoustic metamaterials, and so on.
